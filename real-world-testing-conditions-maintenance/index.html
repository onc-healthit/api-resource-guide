    Real World Testing Condition and Maintenance of Certification

    Health IT developers are required to test the real-world use of APIs.

    The API criteria (§ 170.315(g)(7) through § 170.315(g)(10)) are included under the Real World Testing Condition and Maintenance of Certification requirements of the ONC Cures Act Final Rule in §170.405, which states “A health IT developer with Health IT Module(s) certified to any one or more 2015 Edition certification criteria in § 170.315(b), (c)(1) through (3), (e)(1), (f), (g)(7) through (10), and (h) must successfully test the real world use of those Health IT Module(s) for interoperability (as defined in 42 U.S.C.300jj(9) and § 170.102) in the type of setting in which such Health IT Module(s) would be/is marketed.” More information can be found on the Real World Testing Fact Sheet and Real World Testing Resource Guide.
